Guiding Principles

Sheet P-003

I

Truth to Materials

Let wood be wood and stone be stone. Every material has an inherent character, a grain, a weight, a way of meeting light. The architect's duty is to honor these qualities rather than disguise them.

II

Organic Integration

A building should appear to grow from its site as naturally as a tree from the soil. The roofline should trace the contour of the hill. The garden wall should dissolve into meadow. Architecture and landscape are one continuous thought.

III

The Destruction of the Box

Rooms need not be cages. Through the elimination of the corner post, the cantilevered roof, and the ribbon window, interior space flows freely outward, dissolving the boundary between shelter and sky.

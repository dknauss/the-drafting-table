Our Story

Est. 2008

The Drafting Table was founded in a converted carriage house on the outskirts of Madison, Wisconsin, with a belief that architecture should be as thoughtful as the lives it shelters. We began with residential work: modest houses for families who wanted to live more deliberately within their landscapes.

Over seventeen years, the practice has grown to include civic buildings, cultural spaces, and community gathering places, but the ethos remains unchanged: listen to the land first, draw with precision and care, build with honest materials, and never stop asking whether each decision serves the people who will inhabit the result.

We still draw by hand before we model digitally. We still visit sites at dawn and dusk. We still believe that the best buildings are those that look as if they have always been there, as natural to their place as the trees that surround them.
